Plan Development and Maintenance
Monitoring, Evaluation, and Implementation
The State Emergency Response and Recovery Plan (SERRP) is.
a living document that evolves to reflect changes in policy,
operations, and lessons learned from exercises and real-.
world incidents. Maintaining an up-to-date and effective
plan requires continuous collaboration among state
agencies, local and tribal partners, NGOs, and the private
sector.
Development and maintenance of the SERRP are coordinated.
by the Arizona Department of Emergency and Military Affairs, Division of Emergency
Management (DEMA/EM), but all emergency management partners share responsibility for
ensuring its accuracy and effectiveness..
To ensure the SERRP remains current and operationally effective, DEMA/EM leads a continuous
improvement process that includes:.
Ongoing collaboration with EsF/RsF partners to review and refine the plan.
Regular meetings focused on specific ESFs/RsFs to assess changes in responsibilities,
policies, and operational needs.
Annual plan review by the DEMA/EM Director/designee to incorporate updates based
on new federal/state guidance, emerging threats, and response best practices.
Post-incident and after-action reviews to integrate lessons learned and corrective
actions into plan updates..
Minor administrative updates, such as grammatical corrections, formatting adjustments, or
minor terminology revisions, will be made as needed. Significant revisions will be reviewed,
approved, and redistributed to all agencies and partners named in the SERRP.
All state agencies and departments are responsible for aligning their Emergency Operations
Plans (EOPs) and Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) with the latest SERRP guidance. Local,
county, and tribal partners are encouraged to reference and integrate SERRP elements into
their own emergency planning efforts..
Training and Exercising the Plan
Ensuring that Arizona's emergency management community is prepared to execute the SERRP
requires ongoing training, exercises, and capability assessments..
DEMA/EM provides training, technical assistance, and exercise coordination for:
Local, county, tribal, and state agencies.
Private Sector Partners and NGOs.
