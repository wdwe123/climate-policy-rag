This ISM structure aligns with SEOc functions, resource request processes, and information
flow protocols, ensuring effective incident management.
The SEOc serves as Arizona's central incident.
coordination center, ensuring real-time
information sharing, interagency coordination,.
and operational control during emergencies or
disasters. The SEOC operates at three levels of
activation, depending on the severity and scope
of the incident:
Description Level
SEOC Full Activation
Command and general staff, along with their support staff, ESFs and RsFs, and. Level I
technical specialists, are activated to support a response to a major incident or.
credible threat.
SEoc Heightened Steady State with Enhanced Monitoring
Command and general staff, along with needed EsFs and technical staff, are Level II
partially activated to monitor or support a credible threat, risk, hazard, or large.
scale planned event.
SEOc Steady State & Monitoring Operations
Level III Operations are normal for the SEoc; no incident, specific risk, or hazard has been
identified. The Response Branch conducts normal monitoring.
During activation, the SEOc maintains direct coordination with local, county, tribal, and federal
agencies. Emergency communications are streamlined to ensure rapid resource deployment,
situational updates, and policy adjustments as conditions evolve.
Multi-Jurisdictional Incidents
Incidents may impact multiple counties, tribal nations, and municipalities simultaneously,
requiring a coordinated state response. When requested, the state may provide:.
Technical assistance for response and recovery operations.
State liaisons to facilitate communication between local EOcs and the SEOc.
Deployment of ESF/RsF agency resources to affected areas.
